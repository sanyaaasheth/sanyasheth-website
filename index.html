My experience stretches back to high school when I was the Treasurer of a fundraiser-based theatre company, Arcadia Stage. With the significant amounts of money that would come in through our constant theatrical production, I learned how to organize cash through spreadsheets, send in purchase orders in a timely manner, and excel in time management. Throughout the week, my expectation was to organize the cash by bills, create a deposit sheet, and send it in before our next deposit. On top of that, I was in charge of communicating with the accountant of our company, our director, and the school treasurer to ensure that everything ran smoothly through the accounts. In addition, I would like to highlight a previous job I was a part of -- my own, online dance company. This was a company I created during the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic in order to keep others and myself, entertained. I would spend several hours a week creating dances for young kids, and through zoom, they would learn techniques and a variety of dance styles. Throughout my summer of teaching, I grasped the concept of communication and learned how to think on my feet in case of any conflict -- making me a more flexible and communicative person. Moreover, while my previous positions allowed me to maintain a well-rounded skill set, I would like to further my meticulous and precise work skills, furthering my ability to think on my feet. With that, I could bring a positive attitude to this job position and use that to have an open and easy-to-work-in environment.

I love school!
